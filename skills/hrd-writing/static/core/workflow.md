# HRD Writing Workflow

1. Identify the user's requested output: revision, draft, structure, critique, or journal-fit advice.
2. Detect the task, genre, section, and journal axes from `manifest.yaml`.
3. Preserve the manuscript's claims, citations, constructs, design, sample, and findings.
4. Locate the HRD contribution: problem, theory, constructs, method or argument, contribution, and practice relevance.
5. Improve conceptual precision, paragraph unity, transitions, and claim-evidence alignment.
6. Apply APA 7 rules for citations, tense, numbers, headings, statistics, and cautious claims.
7. Flag unresolved issues that need author verification.

For substantial drafts, first form a one-sentence argument before writing prose.
