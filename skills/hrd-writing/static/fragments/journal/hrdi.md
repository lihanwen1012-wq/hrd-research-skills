# Journal: HRDI

Use for Human Resource Development International orientation.

Emphasize:

- international, cross-cultural, comparative, or globally relevant HRD framing when applicable
- sensitivity to national, institutional, cultural, and organizational context
- avoidance of assuming one country's HRD context is universal
- contribution to HRD scholarship beyond a single local case unless the local case is theoretically meaningful

Fit check:

- Does the manuscript explain the context in ways international readers can understand?
- Are claims bounded to the study setting when needed?
- Is the HRD contribution relevant beyond description of one location or organization?
