# HRD Paper Development Checklist

Use this checklist when building an HRD manuscript from scratch or from rough materials.

## Intake

- What is the HRD topic, problem, or phenomenon?
- What context matters: industry, country, organization type, workers, learners, leaders, or HRD practitioners?
- What theory, framework, or conceptual lens is available?
- What evidence exists: data, findings, tables, figures, literature notes, interview themes, or prior drafts?
- What is the target journal or audience?

## Motivation

- Is the central motivation specific?
- Does it name the HRD gap or problem?
- Is the contribution appropriately bounded?
- Is there enough evidence to support the intended paper?

## Architecture

- Does the introduction lead naturally to the purpose?
- Does the literature review synthesize around constructs, theories, or tensions?
- Do research questions or hypotheses align with design and evidence?
- Does the discussion answer the beginning of the paper?
- Do implications identify who can act and under what limits?

## Evidence

- Is each major claim supported by user evidence or a citation need?
- Are findings separated from interpretation?
- Are causal claims supported by design?
- Are figures and tables mapped to the claims they support?

## Output

- If evidence is incomplete, produce a blueprint or partial draft rather than pretending the manuscript is complete.
- Mark missing details clearly.
- Preserve APA 7 and HRD journal fit guidance.
