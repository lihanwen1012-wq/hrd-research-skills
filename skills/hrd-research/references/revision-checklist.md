# HRD Research Revision Checklist

Use this checklist when revising or evaluating HRD manuscript prose.

## Before Revising

- Identify the section and purpose.
- Note the target audience or journal if provided.
- Preserve all citations, numbers, hypotheses, research questions, participant details, and construct names.
- Ask only if a missing detail prevents responsible revision.

## Clarity

- Does each paragraph have one controlling idea?
- Does the first sentence orient the reader?
- Are key constructs named consistently?
- Are pronouns and referents clear?
- Are transitions logical rather than decorative?

## Argument

- Is the HRD problem clear?
- Is the theoretical mechanism explicit?
- Does the literature synthesize instead of listing studies?
- Does the gap justify the research purpose?
- Are claims proportional to the evidence?

## APA And Social Science Style

- Are citations placed next to supported claims?
- Are tense choices appropriate?
- Are statistical claims preserved and reported cautiously?
- Is causal language justified by the design?
- Is inclusive language used?

## Response Pattern

For most revision requests, respond with:

1. Revised version
2. What changed
3. Items to verify

Keep notes short unless the user asks for detailed explanation.
