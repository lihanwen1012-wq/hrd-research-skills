# HRD Research Visualization Checklist

Use this checklist when creating, reviewing, or interpreting HRD research graphs, charts, figures, and tables.

## Purpose

- Does the visual answer a specific research question or support a specific manuscript claim?
- Is the visual better than a table for this information?
- Is the unit of analysis clear?

## Data Integrity

- Are variable names, groups, sample sizes, and units accurate?
- Are missing values, exclusions, or aggregation choices clear when relevant?
- Is uncertainty shown when the reader needs it to interpret inference?

## HRD Fit

- Are constructs named in HRD language rather than generic business labels?
- Is the workplace, learning, development, performance, or organizational context visible?
- Does the visual avoid implying causality beyond the design?

## APA And Readability

- Are title, labels, notes, abbreviations, and statistics clear?
- Are decimals and scale endpoints consistent?
- Can the visual be understood without relying only on color?
- Is the figure or table simple enough for journal readers to scan?

## Interpretation

- Does the accompanying prose identify the main pattern?
- Does it connect the pattern to theory, research questions, findings, or implications?
- Does it state limits or cautions when needed?
