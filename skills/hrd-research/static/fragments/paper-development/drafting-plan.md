# Paper Development: Drafting Plan

Draft in a sequence that protects argument quality:

1. Confirm motivation and contribution.
2. Build the section blueprint.
3. Draft the introduction and purpose after the paper architecture is clear.
4. Draft theory/literature sections around constructs and mechanisms, not source order.
5. Draft methods from user-provided design details.
6. Draft results/findings only from user-provided data or findings.
7. Draft discussion by returning to research questions, theory, and HRD contribution.
8. Draft implications with evidence-proportional practice recommendations.
9. Draft abstract last.

If inputs are incomplete, provide a structured draft with clear placeholders such as `[insert sample details]` or `[citation needed]`.
