# Paper Development: Motivation

The motivation is the paper's central reason to exist.

For HRD research, define it as one concise claim about:

- the HRD problem or opportunity
- the theoretical or empirical gap
- the workplace, learning, development, performance, or organizational context
- why this study or argument is needed now
- what contribution the paper can make without overclaiming

Good motivation statements are specific and bounded. Avoid broad claims such as "this study fills a gap" without naming the gap, audience, evidence, and HRD relevance.

If the user gives several possible motivations, present 2-4 options with tradeoffs and ask them to choose before drafting the full paper.
