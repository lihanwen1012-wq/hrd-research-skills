# Paper Development: Section Blueprint

Use a section blueprint before drafting long text.

Recommended blueprint table:

| Section | Function | Key Claims | Evidence/Citations Needed | HRD Contribution Link | Drafting Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Typical empirical HRD paper sections:

- Abstract
- Introduction
- Literature review or theoretical framework
- Research questions or hypotheses
- Method
- Results or findings
- Discussion
- Implications for HRD research and practice
- Limitations and future research
- Conclusion

Do not force this structure if the target journal or genre calls for a conceptual, review, or methods-oriented structure.
