# Paper Development: Architecture

Use this to design the whole-paper argument.

Build the architecture as a chain:

1. HRD problem: what is happening in work, learning, development, or performance contexts?
2. Knowledge gap: what prior research does not yet explain, test, integrate, or theorize?
3. Purpose: what the paper does in response.
4. Theory or lens: how the paper explains the issue.
5. Method or argument: how evidence or reasoning is developed.
6. Findings or propositions: what the paper contributes.
7. Discussion: what changes in HRD knowledge.
8. Implications: what HRD scholars or practitioners can do with the contribution.

Check for front-back echo: the discussion and implications should answer the problem and gap introduced at the beginning.
