# Visualization: APA Figure

For APA-style figures:

- Use a concise figure number and italicized title in final prose when possible.
- Make axis labels descriptive and include units or scale endpoints.
- Define abbreviations in the figure note.
- Use direct labels when they reduce legend-hunting.
- Keep colors interpretable in grayscale or for readers with color-vision differences.
- Show uncertainty when inference is central, such as confidence intervals or standard errors.
- Avoid unnecessary gridlines, heavy borders, 3D effects, and chartjunk.

Figure notes should explain what is necessary to read the figure, not restate the full results section.
