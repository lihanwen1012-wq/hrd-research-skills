# Visualization: Chart Choice

Choose the chart from the analytic task:

- Compare categories: bar chart, dot plot, or forest plot.
- Show change over time: line chart or slope chart.
- Show distributions: histogram, density plot, box plot, violin plot, or raincloud plot.
- Show relationships: scatterplot with fitted line, faceting, or marginal summaries.
- Show model estimates: coefficient plot or marginal effects plot.
- Show mediation or moderation conceptually: path diagram or interaction plot.
- Show qualitative themes: matrix, heatmap, evidence table, or concept map.

Prefer dot plots or coefficient plots when exact comparison is important. Avoid 3D charts, pie charts for many categories, and decorative effects that weaken interpretation.

For HRD research, labels should name constructs, samples, contexts, and practical units clearly enough for readers outside the immediate project.
