# Visualization: Narrative

Use when writing manuscript prose about a figure, graph, chart, or table.

Structure interpretation as:

1. Orient the reader to the visual.
2. State the main pattern.
3. Connect the pattern to the HRD research question, theory, or implication.
4. Qualify the claim based on design and uncertainty.

Do not narrate every value. Emphasize the pattern that matters for the argument.

Use cautious language for descriptive or correlational visuals: "shows," "indicates," "is consistent with," "suggests," or "may reflect."
