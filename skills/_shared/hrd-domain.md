# HRD Domain Guidance

Use this reference when the writing task depends on Human Resource Development concepts, journal fit, or field-specific framing.

## HRD Scope

HRD research commonly addresses learning, development, and performance in work and organizational contexts. Relevant areas include:

- workplace learning
- training and development
- training transfer
- organization development
- career development
- leadership development
- employee development
- mentoring and coaching
- performance improvement
- talent development
- adult learning in organizations
- learning culture and climate
- diversity, equity, inclusion, and belonging at work

## HRD Argument Pattern

Strong HRD manuscripts usually make clear:

1. The workplace or organizational problem
2. The theoretical lens or conceptual mechanism
3. The HRD constructs involved
4. The method used to examine the problem
5. The contribution to HRD research
6. The implications for HRD practice

## Common Revision Moves

- Replace broad "business" or "management" framing with HRD-specific relevance when appropriate.
- Connect individual learning and development to organizational context.
- Clarify whether "development" means employee development, leadership development, career development, organization development, or human development more broadly.
- Separate training outcomes from learning outcomes, transfer outcomes, performance outcomes, and organizational outcomes.
- Identify who can act on the findings: HRD scholars, training designers, HRD practitioners, managers, leaders, policymakers, educators, or employees.

## Practice Implications

Good HRD implications are actionable but not exaggerated. They identify:

- what practitioners can do
- under what conditions
- for which population or setting
- with what evidence strength
- what limits or cautions apply

Avoid promising organizational transformation from evidence that supports only modest or context-specific claims.
